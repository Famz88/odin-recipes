Chicken Biryani Recipe


Description

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the royal khansamas of the durbar of Old Delhi, under the Mughal Empire, during the late 16th century of the then Mughal Court.[1] It is made with Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, prawn, fish) or in some cases without any meat, and sometimes, in addition, eggs and potatoes.

Biryani is more popular in East Africa mainly because it is the main food for all kinds of celebrations especially Weddings. Any Wedding without a Biryani it is not complete.


Ingredients


Curry (Rojo)

  • 10 Eggs Hard boiled
  • 1 Kg Full Capone Cut in to 8 pieces
  • 500 ml Vegetable oil
  • 5 Onions
  • 1 tsp Garlic Minced
  • 1 tsp Ginger Minced
  • 1 tbsp Whole pilau mix
  • 4 Tomatoes Cubed
  • 1 Green pepper Finely sliced
  • 100 g Tomato paste
  • 1 Lemon Juiced
  • 500 ml Plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Black pepper

    • Rice

  • 3 cups Basmati rice
  • 6 cups Water Will not be provided with the recipe
  • 1 tsp Yellow food colour
  • 2 tbsp Salt

    • Salad

  • 2 Onions Diced
  • 3 Tomatoes Diced
  • 1 Lemon Juiced
  • 1 Green chilli Chopped (Optional)

Steps

  1. In a deep fat frying pan add in the oil and fry the onions to golden brown.
  2. In a separate pan add 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil (Use the same oil for onions its flavorful) and Sauté the chicken.
  3. Add garlic, ginger and lemon juice and continue to Sauté.
  4. Partly crush the whole pilau mix.
  5. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, yogurt, pilau mix, green pepper and salt.
  6. Stir well for the flavors to coat on the chicken.
  7. Sprinkle the fried onions on top.
  8. Finish by adding the boiled eggs and coriander.
  9. Cover and let it cook for about 20-25 Minutes on low heat until the curry thickens.

Rice

  1. On the other hand, Measure 6 cups of water.
  2. Add in the 3 cups of rice and salt cover and cook until the rice is almost cooked
  3. Sprinkle the diluted yellow food color on the rice and 3 tablespoons of the oil used to fry onions.
  4. Cover with a tight lid then put on a low flame so that the rice can steam and become fluffy.

Salad (Kachumbari)

  1. Dice all the onions, coriander and tomatoes.
  2. Add them into a bowl and mix.
  3. Juice the lemon on the mix.
  4. You can mix in the green chilli.
Tip: Serve the rice with the thick curry on top. Salad & Achari on the side.
Happy Cooking.
